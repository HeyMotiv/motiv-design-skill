Your collab

Collabs are short, structured, and easy to run

A Motiv collab is a 4–8 week activation where you're the inspirer-in-chief. Your followers join a shared journey with you—centered on habits, consistency, and the products you genuinely stand for. You bring your voice (as you do on social), but in a dedicated collaborative space designed for action.

Choose a goal type (steps, workouts, cycling output, etc.)
Choose a duration (4–8 weeks)
Set the participant hold amount (refundable on completion)
Add your guidance: expectations, tips, and weekly prompts

Unlike a traditional brand partnership, a Motiv collab is built around you. There's no external sponsor or forced message—just your voice, the habits you stand for, and the products that supported your journey.

Trust

Designed around trust—the foundation of your relationship

Your audience trusts you. Motiv is built to honor that. Participants always know what they're committing to, and the experience is designed so that following through feels clear, fair, and rewarding.

Follow through, get refunded

Participants who complete the collab get their money back. It's that simple—show up, do the work, and your commitment is returned to you.

Rewards that make it worth it

Beyond getting refunded, participants can qualify for rewards and bonuses—making the experience feel free, or even rewarding, for those who follow through.

Clarity, fairness, and respect

Every collab spells out what it takes to qualify. No fine print, no surprises. If someone falls short, they're treated with dignity—not judgment.

How it works

How Motiv works

Motiv's AI does the heavy lifting—generating a collab draft aligned with your brand, voice, and audience. You refine and approve it, rather than starting from scratch. Creating a collab should feel easy, not like a chore.

1

Create your collab

Motiv's AI generates a collab draft based on your brand, audience, and style. Your role is refinement—not manual setup. You control the voice, the intent, the habits you want to spotlight, and the products you stand for.

2

Rally your audience

Share it with your audience and let the excitement build. As people join, you'll see the collab come to life before it even starts.

3

Lead the experience

Post normally. Encourage momentum. Share your own progress. Motiv supports participants with structure and visibility so they don't need to DM you for clarity.

4

Wrap-up and outcomes

At the end, participants see a self-accounting of their progress. Those who qualify receive their refund (and any applicable rewards). Those who don't qualify can still finish—but their hold is disqualified under the rules.

5

Creator payout

Motiv pays creators based on the collab structure—combining guaranteed components (when applicable) plus performance-based upside.

Earnings

How creators earn on Motiv

Motiv is designed around what you already do—show up, lead, and inspire action. The difference is that your earnings are tied to real participation and leadership, not hype or impressions.

Earnings pillars

Three ways you earn

Paid as people join

As sign-ups hit participation benchmarks, you earn. The more people who join, the more you make—rewarding creators who can inspire action.

Paid for showing up and leading

Post one video a week in your own voice — share what's on your mind, keep your people inspired. That's it.

Upside when collabs perform well

Beyond your base earnings, you participate in the collab's financial upside. Better performance means a bigger payout.

Illustrative example

What this could look like

Exact earnings depend on your audience, collab structure, and participation rates. But here's a realistic scenario to give you a sense of scale.

Example: 6-week cycling challenge

A creator with 200k followers runs a 6-week cycling challenge. 1% of followers join (2,000 participants) at a $40 hold per person.

Join bonus (paid at participation benchmarks) $800
Content (one video per week, 6 weeks) $500

These are illustrative estimates, not guarantees. Actual earnings vary based on collab terms, participation, and completion rates. Motiv is still in development and final economics may differ.
