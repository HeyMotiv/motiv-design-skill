The Approach

Why Not Let AI Design Every Screen?

Modern AI models aren't just trained on dataâ€”they're shaped by RLHF to optimize for perceived quality, which tends to converge on standard design patterns ideal for routine user experiences. Without deliberate creative direction, outputs lack the distinctive brand identity that makes products memorable.

Humans Excel At

  • Making unexpected creative choices that stand out
  • Establishing emotional resonance and brand voice
  • Creating a unique, ownable point of view

AI Excels At

  • Following established patterns with perfect consistency
  • Propagating design decisions to dozens of screens
  • Maintaining quality on screen #75 identical to screen #1

The insight: AI can make mediocre things quickly from scratch. It struggles to make something above average without creative direction. When humans design the key screens with a strong point of view, those decisions become the patterns AI can propagate. You're seeding the system with opinion.

The Challenge

Design at Scale is Hard

Modern apps need dozensâ€”sometimes hundredsâ€”of screens. Traditional approaches force difficult tradeoffs.

Quality Degrades at Scale

Screen #50 doesn't get the same attention as screen #5. Settings pages get less love than onboarding. The details slip.

Consistency Drifts

Multiple designers + time pressure = subtle inconsistencies. Spacing drifts, colors vary, patterns diverge across the app.

AI Alone is Generic

Pure AI generation regresses to the mean. Safe colors, standard layouts, familiar patterns. Nothing memorable or ownable.
