A new kind of workflow

Design what matters most.
AI carries it forward.

Every flow you design encodes hundreds of decisions. Together, they form a complete design system, implicitly. This workflow extracts it, makes it machine-readable, and applies it everywhere and anywhere you want — creating results that are both unique and consistent.

The Problem

AI is changing what designers produce. The role needs to evolve with it.

Building products got easier. Designing the right experience—the flows, the priorities, the details that make it all work—didn't.

01

Designers are being asked whether they're still needed

But the question misses the point. The value of design was never just producing interfaces—it's the decisions behind them.

02

AI can generate interfaces, but it can't decide what to build, what to cut, or how it all connects

Without someone making those calls, AI produces work that looks finished but isn't coherent. The parts don't add up to a product.

03

The gap between "looks done" and "is done" is exactly where design skill lives

Hierarchy, flow, state handling, edge cases, emotional tone—these are the things that separate output from a product that works. That skill doesn't go away because the tools got faster.

The Challenge

Design and AI aren't competing approaches. They're incomplete ones.

Design teams are caught between two constraints.

Design alone: it takes too long or requires too large a team

A real product isn't a handful of hero moments — it's hundreds of states, flows, edge cases, and platform variations that all need to feel intentional. Doing that well either takes a long time or a large, tightly coordinated team. Both are expensive, and coordination problems erode the consistency that makes a product feel whole.

AI alone: it's fast, but it can't break past generic

AI can produce layouts, apply patterns, and generate plausible interfaces quickly. But it doesn't make product decisions — it doesn't know which flow matters most, where to break a pattern for emphasis, how to handle a failure gracefully, or when something technically correct still feels wrong. That last 40% — the hierarchy, pacing, edge cases, and emotional coherence — is where the experience actually comes together.

The Approach

A methodology where designers set the standard and AI carries it forward.

Designers do the work only they can do. AI handles the rest.

01

Designers define the quality bar, craft the critical experiences, and build the system everything else inherits from

The decisions that set the tone, establish patterns, and handle the hardest interactions are made by designers—with full creative energy.

02

AI extends that system across the full product—constrained by real standards, not freestyling

AI works from explicit tokens, component specs, and interaction patterns the designer created. It's not guessing—it's following the system.

03

The result is better work, delivered predictably, with design decisions running through all of it

Every part of the product—whether hand-crafted or generated—reflects the same standards because it all comes from the same source of truth.

How It Works

Not every part of a product needs the same level of design attention.

Some moments define how the product feels — onboarding, core features, emotional peaks. Others just need to follow the rules well. The designer's job isn't to design everything. It's to identify where design energy has the most impact, focus there, and let AI carry the rest using the standards they set. We use a simple classification to make that split clear.

Human-Critical

First impressions, emotional peaks, and conversion moments that define how the product feels.

• Onboarding welcome
• Core dashboard
• Success celebrations
• Paywall / upgrade

Co-Design

AI takes a first pass, then designers refine and tweak until it's right.

• Profile pages
• Detail views
• Form flows
• Navigation patterns

AI-Extended

Systematic, pattern-following work that AI generates from the standards designers set.

• Settings screens
• List views
• Empty states
• Error messages

Example breakdown for a typical product:

25%
Designed by humans
25%
Co-designed: AI first pass, human refined
50%
Generated by AI from the system

Actual split varies by product. Apps with more unique interactions need more human design; apps with more systematic screens (settings, lists, forms) can lean heavier on AI generation.

The Workflow

From Understanding to Handoff

Three phases, each with clear deliverables. Click to expand and see details.

1

Discover & Plan

Understand → Map & Classify → Approve
Human + AI

Understand

Share raw inputs—meeting transcripts, Slack threads, product briefs. AI helps extract and structure the key elements.

  • Product vision and success criteria get clarified
  • User types and their goals are identified
  • Core flows are mapped at a high level
  • Constraints and non-negotiables are captured
DISCOVERY.md Product vision User types
AI

Map & Classify

AI generates a complete map of every screen the product needs. Each screen gets classified based on design impact.

  • Every screen inventoried with purpose and emotional intent
  • Classified: Human-Critical, Co-Design, AI-Extended
  • Flow connections mapped (what leads to what)
  • Required states identified (loading, empty, error)
PRODUCT-MAP.md screens/inventory.json
Review

Approve Plan

Review the map, adjust classifications as needed, assign designers to critical screens. These are recommendations, not rules.

2

Design & Iterate

Design → Extract → Generate → Review
Human

Design Key Screens

Designers focus full creative energy on the screens that define the product's soul. Work in Figma or code—whatever feels right.

  • Design Human-Critical screens first (onboarding, core features, emotional peaks)
  • Move to Co-Design screens (important flows, pattern-setters)
  • Establish visual language, component patterns, and emotional tone
  • Make creative choices AI would never make
Figma designs or HTML prototypes
AI

Extract Design System

AI analyzes your screens and extracts everything into a formal system. This captures what you actually did—ensuring the system matches reality.

  • Color tokens extracted with semantic names
  • Typography scale codified
  • Spacing system derived from actual usage
  • Component patterns documented
  • Motion specs proposed (you approve or adjust)
tokens/colors.json tokens/typography.json components/*.md
AI

Generate Remaining Screens

Using the extracted design system, AI generates all AI-Extended screens. These follow your patterns perfectly because they're derived from your actual designs.

  • Settings screens generated with correct spacing and typography
  • Detail views follow established component patterns
  • All states handled (loading, empty, error)
  • Every screen feels like part of the same family
prototype/screens/*.html All states implemented
Review

Review & Iterate

Review AI output, adjust tokens if needed, regenerate. Loop until satisfied.

3

Handoff

Package Specs → Export Tokens → Document
AI

Engineer Handoff

AI packages everything engineers need to implement. No more "what's the padding here?" questions.

  • Design tokens exported (CSS variables, JSON, Tailwind config)
  • Component specs with all variants and states
  • Accessibility requirements (GREEN-LINES)
  • Implementation specs (RED-LINES)
  • Motion timing and easing documented
handoff/tokens.css handoff/GREEN-LINES.md handoff/RED-LINES.md handoff/COMPONENTS.md
It's a Loop, Not a Line

You Can Always Go Back

This workflow isn't waterfall. At every stage, you can iterate, adjust, and refine. The artifacts update together.

✏️ Design key screens
→
⚡ AI fills in the rest
→
👁️ Review
→ ↻

Change a Token

Update the primary color in the design system → Regenerate affected screens → Review the changes. The whole app updates together.

Add a Screen

Realize you need a new screen? Add it to the inventory → Classify it → Generate or design it. The map updates automatically.

Rethink a Flow

User testing reveals a problem? Restructure the flow in the map → Affected screens are flagged → Regenerate or redesign as needed.

Promote a Screen

An AI-generated screen needs more love? Move it to Co-Design, design it yourself, and the extracted patterns can improve future generations.

For Designers

What changes — and what doesn't

The core work is the same: understanding problems, making decisions, shaping how a product feels. What changes is where you spend your time.

Before: Time Spent On

  • Producing every view at varying quality
  • Manually maintaining consistency across the product
  • Creating specs that get outdated immediately
  • Repeating established patterns by hand
  • Catching edge cases late in the process
→

After: Time Spent On

  • Making the critical decisions that shape the product
  • Designing the key moments exceptionally well
  • Defining the standards the whole system inherits
  • Reviewing AI output and directing refinements
  • Solving the problems that don't have patterns yet

The shift: Designers work at a higher altitude — focusing on the decisions and standards that matter most, while AI carries that work across the full product. The skill is the same. The leverage is different.
